Candied yams are a classic comfort food and a beloved staple at holiday tables, especially during Thanksgiving. Made with tender slices of yams coated in a buttery, sweet glaze infused with warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg, this dish is a perfect balance of richness and sweetness. Often baked until caramelized and golden, candied yams bring a cozy, nostalgic flavor to any meal. Whether paired with savory roasts or enjoyed on their own, they are a timeless recipe that adds a touch of indulgence to every bite.

Ingredients

  • 4 large yams, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup water
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 190Â°C.
  2. Prepare the yams: Arrange the sliced yams in a single layer in a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  3. Make the candied mixture: In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the brown sugar, granulated sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger (if using), and a pinch of salt. Cook until the sugars dissolve and the mixture is smooth, about 2 to 3 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Coat the yams: Pour the candied mixture evenly over the yams, ensuring all pieces are coated. Add the water or orange juice to the dish, pouring around the edges to prevent diluting the glaze directly on the yams.
  5. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil, gently stir the yams to ensure even coating, and bake uncovered for another 15 minutes or until the yams are tender and caramelized.
  6. Serve: Let the candied yams cool slightly before serving. They are perfect as a side dish for holiday feasts or any comforting meal.
